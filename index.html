Onion Farming

According to the Food and Agriculture Organizations (FAO) 2014 report 50 percent of the red onions in Kenya are imported from Tanzania, making the red bulb onion one of the most attractive commercial farming practices that a farmer can do.

Stages of onion growth

  • Germination
  • Seedling
  • Vegetative growth
  • Bulb formation
  • Maturation stage
  • Harvesting

Germination:

The onion germination stage is the process of the onion seed developing into a new onion plant.The onion germination stage can take anywhere from a few days to a few weeks to complete. The exact time it takes depends on several factors including the temperature, the humidity and type of soil. The higher the temperature the higher the humidity , the faster the onion germination stage will be. While the lower the temperature and the lower the humidity the slower the onion germination stage will be.

Seedling:

During this stage, the onion plant will grow from a tiny seed into a small, green plant. The onion plant will grow steadily during this stage, and it will be important to water it. That is why at Kasyuma's Farm Services we have a borehole where we get our fresh water and use drip irigation to water the plants. The onion plant will reach its full height during the seedling stage. In order to produce large, healthy bulbs, the plant must grow quickly during this stage.

Vegetative stage:

In this stage the onion plant begin to form bulbs.

Bulb formation stage:

The plant will form a bulb which will continue to grow in size until it is ready to harvest.

Maturation Stage:

Onion maturation stage happens at the end of the onion's life cycle after all leaves have wilted away and gone brown and have fallen to the ground but not detached from the bulb.

Harvesting stage:

Once the onions have reached the harvesting stage, it is time to begin the process of harvestnig them. They are havested by uprooting them then cuting the roots and leaves. They are then sorted according to sizes and packed.
